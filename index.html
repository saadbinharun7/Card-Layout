Sundarbans ★★★★

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the world’s largest mangrove forest, spanning 6,000+ sq km across Bangladesh and India. Home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger, it teems with wildlife like spotted deer, saltwater crocodiles, and rare river dolphins. Its labyrinth of tidal rivers, mudflats, and dense forests thrives with sundari trees (hence its name) and acts as a natural barrier against cyclones. Tanguar Haor, located in Sunamganj, Bangladesh, is a UNESCO Ramsar Site and one of the country’s most vital wetland ecosystems. Known as the "Amazon of Bangladesh," it features floating gardens (baira), lotus fields, and stilted villages.
